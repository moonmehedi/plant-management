Diseases of rice – diversity, dynamics and control

10 August 2021
10 August 2021

This virtual issue of PLANT PATHOLOGY contains a review and thirteen original articles on viral, nematode, bacterial and fungal diseases of rice. These diseases are of global importance and have a significant impact on the production of rice, which is the most widely consumed staple food for over half of the world's human population. The work includes a review on an emerging virus in East Asia, Southern rice black-streaked dwarf virus, followed by a number of articles that highlight population structures in rice pathogens, the influence of weeds and crop residues on these, and the roles of key genes in bacterial and fungal pathogenicity. There are finally four articles demonstrating approaches for control of rice diseases through the use of endophytic and plant growth promoting rhizobacteria, and applications of L-histidine and melatonin. We hope that bringing these papers together into a single virtual issue will help to highlight the significant recent research on rice diseases, and provide a valuable resource.
