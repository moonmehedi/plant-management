Taro is a tropical staple crop with a starchy tuber. To grow it:

1. Choose a tropical location.
2. Prepare well-drained, loamy soil.
3. Plant corms or shoots.
4. Keep the field flooded or moist.
5. Use organic fertilizers.
6. Weed regularly.
7. Control pests and diseases.
8. Harvest carefully in 8-12 months.
9. Wash and peel tubers.
10. Store in a cool, dry place.

Taro is versatile and rich in carbohydrates and dietary fiber.
